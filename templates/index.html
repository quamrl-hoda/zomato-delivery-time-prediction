Model Pipeline

End-to-end ML pipeline from raw features to predicted delivery time.

01
Raw Input Data
15 features collected at order time — numerical, categorical (nominal + ordinal), and passthrough fields.
4 numerical 7 nominal 2 ordinal 2 passthrough
↓
02
ColumnTransformer — Preprocessor
Three parallel transformers applied simultaneously, with remainder cols passed through unchanged.
MinMaxScaler
age · ratings · pickup_time_minutes · distance
OneHotEncoder
weather · type_of_order · type_of_vehicle · festival · city_type · is_weekend · order_time_of_day
drop="first" · handle_unknown="ignore"
OrdinalEncoder
traffic [low→jam] · distance_type [short→very_long]
unknown_value=−1 · missing=−999
Passthrough
vehicle_condition · multiple_deliveries
↓
03
Target Encoding — PowerTransformer
Applied to time_taken (target only) during training to normalise the skewed distribution. At inference, predictions are inverse-transformed back to original minutes scale.
Fitted on y_train inverse_transform at inference
↓
04
StackingRegressor — Ensemble
Four base estimators trained in cross-validation folds. Their out-of-fold predictions are used as meta-features for a final linear meta-learner.
LightGBM
Gradient boosted trees · fast · handles categoricals
Random Forest
Bagged decision trees · low variance
Decision Tree
Single tree · interpretable base learner
Linear Regression meta-learner
Combines base model outputs into final prediction
↓
05
Inference Output
Raw stacking prediction is inverse-transformed through PowerTransformer to produce the final estimate in minutes.
time_taken (minutes)
